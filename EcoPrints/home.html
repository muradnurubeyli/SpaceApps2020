About us

01

EcoPrints is an eco-friendly social media platform that both educate and raise peoples awareness about ecological problems and pursue them for more eco friendly, recyclable lifestyle. In our app people not only could choose and complete various ecological challenges, such as recycling paper, walking or riding instead of driving, or getting energy only from renewable methods like solar, wind, water energy but also they can challenge their friends and make them eco friendly as well.

So we decided to reward our users as well- when they complete challenges they will earn “green feet” and depending on challenge’s importance the amount of “green feet” will vary. Users could exchange these feet for various rewards such as discounts or free products from famous shops and sites.

01 Problem

Air pollution

Air pollution is the most crucial, if not among the most crucial ecological problems of 21st century. Air pollution affects every aspect of human life negatively; it causes temperatures to rise up significantly, hardens breathing, causes severe illness, pierces ozone layer and so on. According to Nasa CO2 concentration in atmosphere has risen by 47% since the industrial revolution begun and according to WHO air pollution accounts for 29% of all deaths and disease from lung cancer, 17% of all deaths and disease from acute lower respiratory infection, 24% of all deaths from stroke, 25% of all deaths and disease from ischaemic heart disease, 43% of all deaths and disease from chronic obstructive pulmonary disease. It also states that nearly 90% of the 4.2 million premature deaths due to ambient air pollution occurred in low- and middle-income countries, with the greatest burden in the WHO Western Pacific and South-East Asia regions. By these numbers its clear that this problem must be addressed quickly



02 Problem

Global Warming

One of the most disturbing ecological problems- Global warming. It causes annual temperatures to rise, glaciers and arctic ice to melt, it creates greenhouse effect, it also results in habitat loss for several species and rising sea levels, severe droughts. Several factors, including burning fossil fuels and fuel powered vehicles, agricultural pesticides(that releases nitrous oxides and methane to atmosphere, factories and etc accounts for global warming. According to Nasa’s ground and satellite data sea level has risen nearly 250mm from 1880 to 2020 and continue to increase by 3,3mm per year. Also according Nasa ICESat and ICESat-2 satellites’ data some major arctic ice losses occurred in Greenland and Antarctic. Also Nasa states that Arctic ice its now declining at the rate of 12.85% per decade and it has lost approximately 3 million sq km of ice in last 40 years. Antarctica on the other hand loses approximately 127 billion metric tons of ice per year. Furthermore Greenland and Antarctica have lost 4910 and 2345 gigatonnes ice in last 18 years and are continue to lose 279 and 148 gigatonnes per year respectively. Another Nasa data shows that global temperature anomaly has risen by approximately 1 Celsius from 1880.



03 Problem

Deforestation

Deforestation is among the most crucial ecological problems that humans face in this century . Several factors including, natural forest fires, human activities led to major deforestations across globe. Deforestation has also have severe outcomes such as soil erosions, destructed habitats for both people and animals, disruption of water cycles, increased greenhouse gas emissions and so on. According to WWF up to 420 million hectares of forest could be lost till 2030 if deforestation trends continues. It also states that tropical forests alone holds nearly 228-247 gigatonnes of carbon emissions-more than 7 times of amount emitted by human activities in a year and deforestation is responsible for 15% of all greenhouse gas emissions. As we mentioned before WWF mentions that nearly 1.25 billion people around the world rely on forests for shelter, livelihoods, water, fuel, and food security and 750 million people (approximately one-fifth of total rural population) live in forests.
